This form is more compact than pre-screening and asks for:

You will need information about all the applicants whose name will appear later on the lease agreement.

‚öñÔ∏è Know the Boundaries

It is important to note that under the Ontario Human Rights Code, landlords cannot reject tenants because of:
- Race, religion, ancestry, or place of origin
- Citizenship or refugee status
- Gender, sexual orientation, or pregnancy
- Family or marital status
- Disability or age
- Receiving public assistance (like ODSP or OW)

Instead, landlords may consider:
- Income (but not income ratios ‚Äî more on that below)
- Credit checks and references
- Rental history
- Guarantors or co-signers

Also, while you can ask about pets, unless the property is a condo with specific rules, you cannot legally prohibit tenants from having pets once the lease is signed.

Performing initial background checks

After some back and forth, once you have enough information and respective consents from candidates it‚Äôs time to validate the details.

  • üßæ Cross-check pre-screening answers with the documents you get from candidates. Are they consistent?

    • Does tenant initial income answer matches what you see from their employment letter? (e.g., employment letter should match income band and tenure).

  • üßç Contact previous landlords and ask few questions about the Applicant:

    • Would they rent to the person again?

    • Is the company real and seem legit?
    • Can they confirm the position and income?

Chances are by this stage you may have eliminated number of candidates due to various discrepancies and red flags, saving money on further steps.

Running paid background checks

Stressing again the importance of you having consent from every person that you will be running background checks for. Got it in place? Good.

First check with you realtor or lawyer - they might be able to help with this step. Barring that, now is time to take out your wallet and order the paid background checks.

Remember in Ontario landlord cannot charge application fees or ask candidates to pay for the background or criminal checks.
